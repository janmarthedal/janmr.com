# CV Improvement Suggestions

Notes from a review of `content/me/cv.njk` (2026-06-10), roughly in order of impact.

## ~~1. The current role is the thinnest entry~~ ✓

"Helping the company scale and stabilize its backend architecture" is one vague
line, while jobs from 2006 get full paragraphs. Readers weight the top entry most
heavily — add the tech stack (Node.js? TypeScript?), what "scale and stabilize"
concretely means, and ideally one specific thing built or fixed since August 2025.

## 2. The detail balance is inverted overall

TracTrac and Netop (2004–2011) get the richest descriptions, including details
like team size and part-time arrangements that no longer matter much. Trim the
pre-2012 entries to 1–2 lines each and spend those words on the last three roles
instead.

## 3. Add a short profile line at the top

Unusual and attractive combination: Ph.D. in applied mathematics plus 20 years of
software engineering across simulation, backend, and full-stack work. One or two
sentences under the name would frame everything that follows.

## 4. Duties vs. outcomes

Most entries describe what was worked on, not what came of it. Even without hard
numbers, phrasing like "designed the data ingestion pipeline that handled X" or
"reduced/enabled Y" lands better than "been involved in data ingestion projects."

## 5. ~~Inconsistent verb style~~ ✓

Entries mix "Working on…", "Worked mainly on…", "Been involved in…", and
"Implemented new functionality and fixing bugs" (Kapow — a genuine tense error).
Pick one convention, e.g. past-tense verbs for past roles, present for the
current one.

## 6. ~~Small consistency nits~~ ✓

- "(Working Remote.)" at HifiShark vs. "(Working remote.)" at Design Science —
  and capitalizing mid-sentence "Remote" is wrong either way.
- "Syosil" in the heading but "SyoSil" in the body text (their site uses SyoSil).
- The resolvent entry's "democratization" is jargon — "stand-alone simulation
  applications so non-specialists can run analyses themselves" says it plainly.
- The Kapow link (`http://kapowsoftware.com`) likely no longer resolves since the
  Kofax acquisition; check old links generally (dessci.com now redirects to Wiris).

## 7. Consider a "Skills" or "Projects/Writing" section

Technologies are scattered through prose (Node.js, C++, Java, Python, C#, Vue,
Django…), so a scanner can't get them at a glance. The blog, the math/CS writing,
and open-source work on GitHub are differentiators the CV never mentions — even
one line under a "Projects" heading would help.

## 8. Education could carry keywords

"Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics" with the thesis title is good, but adding the area
(numerical analysis, PDEs, Krylov methods) as plain text helps both human readers
and keyword filters.

---

Items 1–3 (structure) matter most; the rest is polish.
